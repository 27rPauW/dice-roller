Cornerstone Transformations · Three Decades of Scale

McKinsey Digital

Sector: Professional Services
$1B+
World-Leading Digital Consulting Firm Built
01 · The Challenge

Outdated Consulting Offer

The world's most prestigious strategy firm had no credible digital or technology capability. Clients were turning to specialist digital consultancies and systems integrators for the transformation work that increasingly defined their strategic agendas.

The imperative: build a world-class digital practice from scratch within a partnership culture that prized consensus, intellectual rigour, and long-established ways of working.

02 · The Pivot

A New Global Business Unit

Founded and scaled McKinsey Digital as an entirely new global business unit, integrating strategy, design, engineering, and delivery into a single practice. Recruited thousands of technologists, designers, and digital operators into a firm that had never hired outside the traditional consulting model.

Pioneered a hybrid model that combined McKinsey's strategic rigour with hands-on build-and-operate capability, enabling clients to move from boardroom ambition to working product in weeks rather than months.

03 · The Outcome

World Leading Digital Consulting at Scale

  • £1bn revenue (10% of McKinsey) from a standing start
  • Highest rated digital consulting firm globally
  • Built the platform that would go on to become half of McKinsey's business

Lloyds Banking Group

Sector: Financial Services
37%
Increase in Income Per Employee in 5 Years
01 · The Challenge

Manual Legacy Friction

Britain's largest retail bank operated with fragmented legacy systems that created friction across customer touchpoints. Manual processes slowed service delivery, with simple transactions taking days instead of minutes.

The imperative: transform operational efficiency while maintaining the trust of millions of customers and navigating complex regulatory requirements.

02 · The Pivot

Digital Transformation Program

Designed and implemented a £1bn+ program to upgrade digital channels, automate paper-based processes and modernize the technology estate while maintaining operational stability. Established agile delivery models using an 'internal startup' approach.

View Technical Details +

Platform Architecture: Decomposed monolithic systems into modular services, enabling independent scaling and deployment.

API-First Design: Created standardized interfaces across banking operations, reducing integration complexity and enabling faster feature delivery.

Operational Excellence: Automated key workflows including fraud detection, refund processing, and customer onboarding, reducing processing times from days to seconds.

03 · The Outcome

Lower costs, happier customers

  • 49.3% cost-to-income ratio achieved, demonstrating operational efficiency at scale
  • 15.7M active digital users across mobile and online channels
  • Market-leading NPS scores in UK retail banking customer satisfaction

UK Government

Sector: Public Sector
£45B
AI Savings Identified, £1.2B Banked
01 · The Challenge

Fragmented systems and AI Pilots

The UK civil service operated across hundreds of disparate systems with limited interoperability. Digital capability varied dramatically across departments, creating inconsistent citizen experiences and duplicated effort. AI pilots were proliferating without effective controls.

The imperative: establish a unified digital and AI strategy for UK government, and the organisation to deliver it, while managing £6B+ annual technology spend across 80+ departmental CIOs.

02 · The Pivot

Renewed ambition, strengthened capabilities and controls

Launched a Six-Mission Roadmap to modernize government digital infrastructure. Established cross-government standards, shared platforms, and unified data practices while building digital capability across the senior civil service.

View Technical Details +

Shared Platforms: Created reusable services (e.g., GOV.UK) that departments could adopt instead of building bespoke solutions.

Data Standards: Established unified data architecture and governance frameworks across government departments.

Digital Upskilling: Led comprehensive capability-building program for senior civil servants, ensuring leadership understood the strategic implications of technology decisions.

03 · The Outcome

Modernised services, reduced AI risk

  • £45bn annual savings potential from AI identified
  • 90% of senior civil servants digitally upskilled through structured capability programs
  • 21 of top 75 high-volume services modernized for improved citizen experience
  • AI policy adopted across the public sector

LEGO Brands

Sector: Consumer Products & Media
$9B+
Revenue Doubled in 5 Years through Digital Scaling
01 · The Challenge

Overcoming Physical Gravity

For decades, LEGO's value was locked in physical bricks and third-party retail distribution. While the brand was iconic, the "Digital Play" space remained fragmented, and the technology stack was a collection of legacy systems that could not support the speed of modern consumer expectations or real-time data integration.

The imperative: Transform a physical-first manufacturing giant into a digital-native powerhouse while maintaining the "Play" essence. This required a total architectural reset—moving from technology as a support function to technology as the primary driver of the business model.

02 · The Pivot

A digital reinvention across three vectors

Vector 1: The Efficiency Engine (Core Operations)
Streamlined operations using a modular backbone centred on LEGO ID identity layer and full migration to cloud. This eliminated friction and provided a 360-degree view of the consumer. AI was deployed to optimise demand forecasting and marketing spend.

Vector 2: Top-Line Growth (D2C & Hybrid Play)
Scaled LEGO.com into a powerful direct-to-consumer platform (now ~20% of sales), strengthening consumer relationships and margins.

Vector 3: Step-Out Business (LEGO Digital Play)
Launched a JV with Epic Games and then LEGO Digital Play, a new 'step-out' business unit, to build digital-first relationships with consumers.

View Technical Details +

Platform Modernization: Established a global LEGO ID backbone and migrated the entire infrastructure to a cloud-native architecture to ensure scalability and data consistency.

Operating Model: Reorganized the technology function into a consistent Agile delivery model, shifting from project-based funding to value-stream-based engineering teams.

Governance & Velocity: Implemented a Time to Value framework where tech investments are audited for bottom-line impact on a quarterly basis, ensuring engineering efforts remain tightly coupled with financial outcomes.

03 · The Outcome

A digital platform that enables market-leadership

  • Revenue Doubling: The digital transformation underpinned a 2x increase in revenue over five years.
  • Upgraded Leadership: The transformation's success catalyzed a strategic "Board Upgrade," bringing in digital experts from global leaders like Google and Supercell to steer the next phase of growth.
  • Efficiency Gains: Reached a new benchmark in Time to Value, with tech teams now delivering measurable bottom-line impact every 90 days.

The Willmott Transformation Architecture
REF: WILLMOTT_01 COORD: 51.5074° N
Principle 01

Strategy Over Silicon

There is no "AI Strategy." Technology is a strategic enabler to a new business reality, not a standalone goal. Successful transformation begins with business model clarity, not vendor selection.

REF: WILLMOTT_02 COORD: 40.7128° N
Principle 02

Threat = Opportunity

Treat AI and emerging tech as fundamental threats to the current value chain and primary opportunities for architectural reconfiguration. Disruption is not optional—it's inevitable.

REF: WILLMOTT_03 COORD: 55.9533° N
Principle 03

Transformation Moves at the Speed of the Board

Successful transformation requires a Board that moves as fast as the market. By mastering the technological landscape, leadership evolves from passive oversight to active stewardship—ensuring that every digital investment is governed for risk and optimized for maximum enterprise value.

REF: WILLMOTT_04 COORD: 35.6762° N
Principle 04

The Velocity Flywheel

Abandon "Big Bang" implementations. Start with short-term quick wins to build political and financial capital, then increase scope progressively. Momentum compounds faster than perfection.

REF: WILLMOTT_05 COORD: 1.3521° S
Principle 05

The Human Constraint

Barriers to success are rarely technical; they are almost always rooted in culture, governance, and organizational design. Technology enables change—humans drive it.

REF: WILLMOTT_06 COORD: 37.7749° N
Principle 06

Triple Value Capture

Architect for three simultaneous outcomes: Efficiency Savings, Top-Line Growth, and New Standalone Businesses. Transformation that only cuts costs is incomplete.

REF: WILLMOTT_07 COORD: 40.7128° N
Principle 07

Growth Demands Guardrails

Growth without governance is liability in waiting. As AI reshapes operations, markets, and ethics, Boards must govern operational integrity, strategic discipline, and ethical stewardship with the same rigour they apply to financial risk.

Capturing Value : Three Growth Vectors

VECTOR_1

Core Efficiency

Digitizing the core to slash legacy friction and failure demand. Automate manual processes, eliminate redundant systems, and free up resources for strategic initiatives.

Example: Lloyds Banking Group cut operating costs by 17% in 3 years through platform modernization and process automation.
VECTOR_2

Top-Line Growth

Owning the direct customer relationship to drive new revenue. Build digital channels that enhance customer intimacy and unlock previously inaccessible market segments.

Example: LEGO doubled sales in 5 years through a unified digital ecosystem.
VECTOR_3

New Business

Reconfiguring the value chain to launch AI-native divisions or standalone platforms. Create entirely new revenue streams that didn't exist in the legacy model.

Example: McKinsey spawned McKinsey Digital and grew it to $1bn+ in 7 years.

Governing Pace : Three Resilience Vectors

VECTOR_A

Operational Integrity

Securing the foundational engine to eliminate systemic fragility. Implement rigorous oversight of AI model drift, data provenance, and technical debt. As the core becomes more efficient and automated, these guardrails ensure that "lean" doesn't become "brittle," protecting the organization's license to operate.

Example: JPMorgan Chase utilizes a "Model Risk Management" framework that governs thousands of algorithms, ensuring that efficiency-driving AI doesn't create unmanaged financial or systemic exposure.
VECTOR_B

Strategic Discipline

Governing the pace of expansion to protect brand equity and execution capacity. Establish parameters that ensure growth is sustainable and market-permissioned. These guardrails prevent "strategic drift"—the risk of chasing top-line revenue at the cost of the core brand promise or exceeding the organization's ability to deliver quality at scale.

Example: Nike's shift to a direct-to-consumer (DTC) model succeeded because they maintained strict channel discipline, cutting off dilutive partners to ensure their brand premium remained intact during rapid digital growth.
VECTOR_C

Ethical Stewardship

Defining the ethical frontier to prevent the accumulation of "Ethical Debt." Architect governance frameworks for algorithmic transparency, data sovereignty, and bias mitigation. By governing the "New Business" frontier today, the Board prevents future liabilities that could damage the company's reputation or invite regulatory intervention.

Example: Salesforce established an Office of Ethical and Humane Use to oversee product development, ensuring that new AI-driven business models are built on a foundation of trust that survives evolving global regulations.
