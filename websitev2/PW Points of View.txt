a) Strategy

1. The Valuation Gap: Market cap currently rewards "AI potential," but long-term value only accrues to those who close the gap between hype and operational execution.
2. Commoditization: As AI lowers barriers to entry, brand defensibility must shift from "features" to "proprietary data moats" and hyper-personalized customer intimacy.
3. The Deployment Lag: Success is a marathon of organizational change disguised as a sprint of technological adoption.

b) Operations

1.The End-to-End Mandate: AI is not a "plug-in." ROI is only realized when processes are re-engineered from the ground up, not just incrementally improved.
2. Structural Role Redefinition: If AI doesn't fundamentally change your headcount strategy and job descriptions, you haven't truly adopted it.
3. The Plumbing Tax: You cannot build a 21st-century intelligence layer on top of 20th-century "spaghetti" architecture.

c) Technology

1. Architecture over Apps: Stop buying tools; start building an ecosystem that treats data as the primary fuel and models as interchangeable parts.
2. The Agility Premium: Modular architecture is the only hedge against vendor lock-in and the blistering pace of model obsolescence.

d) Governance & Risk

1. Fiduciary AI Oversight: AI risk is a governance failure, not a technical one. Boards must own the integrity of the algorithms that drive their business.
2. Shadow AI Proliferation: The greatest risk to intellectual property isn't a hack; it’s the invisible, unmanaged use of public AI by a workforce seeking efficiency.
